Privacy Policy

Forwarded Cleaner (Android) · Last updated: 24 May 2026

Short version: Forwarded Cleaner scans the photos and videos on your device to help you find and remove duplicates and forwarded media. Your photos, videos, and any data derived from them stay on your phone and are never uploaded to any server. The only network use is to fetch ads from Google AdMob and to send anonymous crash reports.

1. Who we are

Forwarded Cleaner (package com.cleanphone.mediacleaner) is developed and published by Kishore Geddam, an individual developer. References to "we", "us", or "the developer" in this policy mean Kishore Geddam.

Contact for any privacy question, data request, or complaint: geddam.naidu@gmail.com.

2. What the app does on your device

Forwarded Cleaner reads the photos and videos in your device's media library (and, optionally, folders you explicitly grant access to) so it can:

  • Show you how much space each source (Camera, WhatsApp, Downloads, etc.) is using.
  • Detect visually-similar or duplicate images and videos using on-device hashing.
  • Let you preview, compare side-by-side, or permanently delete files you select.

All of this happens on your device. We do not copy, upload, read, or transmit your photos, videos, file names, file paths, hashes, or thumbnails to any server we control. We do not sell any data — we do not have any data to sell.

3. Permissions the app requests, and why

  • READ_MEDIA_IMAGES, READ_MEDIA_VIDEO, READ_MEDIA_VISUAL_USER_SELECTED (Android 13+) and READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE (Android 10–12) — to enumerate photos and videos in your media library so the app can analyse and display them.
  • INTERNET and ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE — to request banner / interstitial ads from Google AdMob and to send anonymous crash and analytics events.
  • com.google.android.gms.permission.AD_ID — required by Google Play services on Android 13+ for the Google Mobile Ads SDK to read your advertising ID. You can reset or delete this ID at any time in your device settings (Settings → Privacy → Ads).
  • FOREGROUND_SERVICE, FOREGROUND_SERVICE_DATA_SYNC and POST_NOTIFICATIONS — so that long-running scans and deletions can run reliably without being killed by the system, and so we can show progress in a notification.

You can revoke any of these permissions at any time from the system Settings → Apps → Forwarded Cleaner → Permissions. The app will gracefully handle missing permissions and tell you what it cannot do as a result.

4. Data we collect ourselves

None. We do not operate a backend server. We do not have an account system, sign-in, sync, or cloud backup. We do not collect names, email addresses, phone numbers, contacts, location, IMEI, IP addresses, precise device identifiers, or any media metadata. We do not place marketing cookies of our own (there is no website-based product).

5. Data collected by third-party SDKs we use

We embed a small number of well-known SDKs from Google. Each one collects a limited set of diagnostic / advertising data governed by Google's own privacy policies, which apply in addition to this one.

5.1 Google AdMob (advertising)

The app shows a banner ad on the home dashboard. Future versions may add interstitial ads after cleanup runs. AdMob may collect your advertising ID, coarse device information, IP address, ad interaction events, and a general region/country signal in order to serve ads and combat fraud. See Google's advertising privacy policy.

If you are in a region where consent is required (EEA, UK, Switzerland), the app will show Google's User Messaging Platform (UMP) consent form before any personalised ad is requested. You can change your choice later from inside the app.

5.2 Firebase Crashlytics (crash reporting)

When the app crashes, Crashlytics sends an anonymous crash report so we can fix bugs. The report contains the stack trace, device model, Android version, app version, the type of crash, and an automatically-generated installation ID. It does not contain your photos, videos, file names or file contents. See Firebase's privacy and security information.

5.3 Google Analytics for Firebase (basic usage metrics)

We use Google Analytics for Firebase to understand which screens are used and how many cleanups succeed, so we can improve the app. Events are attached to an anonymous Firebase installation ID, not to your personal identity. We do not log photo/video metadata as analytics parameters.

6. In-app purchases

Forwarded Cleaner is currently free with ads and does not offer in-app purchases. If we add a paid "Pro" tier in a future version, this section will be updated to describe it and purchases will be processed entirely by Google Play Billing — we will never see or store your payment details.

7. Children's privacy

Forwarded Cleaner is not directed at children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will take appropriate action.

8. Your rights

Because Forwarded Cleaner does not collect or store your personal data on any server we control, there is no personal-data record for us to export or erase. You can:

  • Revoke media permissions at any time in Android Settings.
  • Reset or delete your advertising ID in Android Settings → Privacy → Ads.
  • Uninstall the app, which removes all on-device data the app keeps (its small Room database of "Keep forever" exclusions and any cached thumbnails).
  • Email geddam.naidu@gmail.com to request that we forward any further deletion requests to our SDK providers (AdMob, Firebase).

Residents of the EEA, UK, Switzerland, California, and other regions with applicable data-protection laws have the right to access, correct, delete, or object to processing of any personal data that third-party SDKs collect through this app. Such requests are handled by those providers directly — see the links in Section 5.

9. Data retention

We retain no user data on our own servers because we operate no servers. Data held by third-party SDKs is retained according to their own retention policies (typically 14 months for advertising data, 90 days for Crashlytics symbolicated crashes).

10. International transfers

When third-party SDKs (Google) process the limited data described above, that processing may take place on servers located outside your country, including in the United States. Those transfers are covered by the providers' own standard contractual clauses and certifications.
